Why St Francis de Sales?

Introduction to the Devout Life was written for Christians living ordinary lives in the world. St Francis does not ask the reader to imitate a monastery, abandon legitimate duties or manufacture religious intensity. He teaches that devotion must be shaped to a person’s actual vocation, responsibilities, health and circumstances.

What may grow from the study?

The hoped-for fruits are not perfect performance or a dramatic reinvention. They are clearer judgement, more faithful prayer, honest repentance, greater gentleness, better government of appetite and speech, steadier fulfilment of duty, healthier relationships and a calmer willingness to begin again.

A leisurely pace

The course is deliberately spacious. A week may be repeated, slowed or paused. Read for understanding rather than speed. Twenty or thirty attentive minutes are better than an exhausting burst followed by abandonment. Completion marks are private aids to orientation, not a measure of holiness.
