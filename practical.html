The Practical Rule

The lessons form the spiritual and intellectual course. This page turns what is discovered there into orderly action: identify the matter, classify it truthfully, write a proportionate plan, carry out the next physical action, review the result, and either close, continue or redirect it.

The governing rule: do not publish private faults, sins, health details, debts or relationship matters. Record those in a private paper notebook or secure personal document. The website teaches the method and supplies blank structures.
Daily foundation

Prayer and rising

Rise at 5:00 am. Keep morning, evening and night prayer. Pray the Angelus at noon and again at 6:00 pm. Make time for one Rosary daily, alternating English, Latin and Spanish.

Daily experiment

The pretend commute

Use one 45-minute block each day for the Latin and Spanish Experiments unless a real bus journey provides the block. Suggested split: 20 minutes Latin, 20 minutes Spanish, five minutes updating notes or identifying what the course page needs next.

Fortnight purpose

Preparation, not perfection

Complete urgent repairs, contain unresolved matters, establish workable domestic and spiritual rhythms, and enter the new job with fewer avoidable points of disorder.

The practical learning cycle

From issue to amendment

Use the same cycle for money, home, food, work preparation, relationships, health appointments, project testing or any other practical concern.

  1. Name the matter. Write one plain sentence describing what is wrong or unfinished.
  2. Establish the facts. Record evidence, dates, balances, quantities, rooms, appointments or observable behaviour. Separate fact from fear and interpretation.
  3. Classify it. Is it principally sin, habit, near occasion, dependency or health matter, domestic duty, financial problem, relationship matter, work risk, project problem, or uncertainty?
  4. Define the desired condition. Describe what “sufficiently resolved” would look like. Avoid perfection.
  5. Choose the next physical action. “Sort money” is not an action; “open every account and record the balances” is.
  6. Place it in time. Put the action into the calendar with a realistic duration and any required journey, cost or preparation.
  7. Carry it out. Work from the written instruction rather than renegotiating the task.
  8. Review the evidence. Note what changed, what resisted, and whether the plan addressed the actual cause.
  9. Redirect. Mark complete, continue, schedule, seek help, or deliberately defer. Do not leave matters in vague mental circulation.

20 July–2 August 2026

The Fortnight of Preparation

Each day keeps prayer, the pretend commute, one important practical task, one domestic task and planned free enjoyment. Tuesday is intentionally lighter because of Mass and travel.

Daily fixed points

  • 5:00 Rise, wash, dress, make bed and morning prayer.
  • 5:30–6:15 Latin and Spanish pretend commute, or use that day’s actual bus journey.
  • 12:00 Angelus. On Tuesday, Mass at noon with the journey protected until about 3:00 pm.
  • 18:00 Angelus and evening prayer.
  • Rosary once daily: English, Latin, Spanish, then repeat.
  • Night brief examination, night prayer, prepare tomorrow, devices away and bed early enough to protect the 5:00 rising.

Daily selection rule

  • One spiritual or moral repair.
  • One urgent practical or financial action.
  • One domestic area.
  • One future-proofing action for work or routine.
  • One planned free or very low-cost pleasure.

Not every category needs a large task. On a travel or recovery day, the task may be ten minutes.

Mon 20 July — Inventory and triage

Create the practical register; survey the home and garden; list urgent money, health, relationship, work and administrative matters; choose the first three actions.

Tue 21 July — Mass and a lighter review day

Morning prayer and pretend commute. Travel to noon Mass; protect the return journey until about 3:00 pm. Afterwards complete only one light domestic action and review the register.

Wed 22 July — Money and immediate safeguards

Record balances, bills, debts, subscriptions and expected income. Establish a no-impulse rule, spending limits and a list of free or already-paid-for recreation.

Thu 23 July — Bedroom, laundry and sleep environment

Clear and clean the bedroom, complete laundry, prepare bedding, remove late-night distractions and write the evening shutdown sequence.

Fri 24 July — Kitchen, food inventory and Friday penance

Inventory cupboards, freezer and fridge. Remove unusable food, list what must be used first and begin the six-week food plan. Keep Friday penance.

Sat 25 July — Garden and exterior order

Mow, edge or clear the garden as weather and health allow. Remove waste and identify any job needing tools, cost or later scheduling.

Sun 26 July — Mass, rest and first weekly review

Attend Mass, keep the day lighter, review the first week and select only the unfinished matters that remain genuinely important.

Mon 27 July — Six-week food and household plan

Complete six weeks of meal themes, freezer use, staple requirements, shopping limits and workday lunches. Plan for economy rather than novelty.

Tue 28 July — Relationships, apologies and boundaries

Review any repair that justice or charity genuinely requires. Draft before acting. Distinguish apology, restitution, boundary, patient distance and matters best left alone.

Wed 29 July — New-job conduct and practical preparation

Write the work rule: listen, ask, complete, delay defensive replies, avoid oversharing and escalate proportionately. Check travel, clothing, documents and first-week needs.

Thu 30 July — Experiment project testing

Run one complete Latin and Spanish commute session as a learner and editor. Record friction, missing links, lesson-update effort and what should become a reusable pattern.

Fri 31 July — Confession, moral repair and unfinished essentials

Prepare for and receive confession where available. Complete one act of restitution or practical repair. Use the remaining work block only for urgent unfinished essentials.

Sat 1 August — Full systems rehearsal

Live the intended workday rhythm: 5:00 rising, prayer, pretend commute, prepared meals, domestic reset and early night. Note where the plan fails in practice.

Sun 2 August — Quiet preparation

Mass, Rosary, food and clothing preparation, final calendar review and rest. No ambitious catch-up programme. Anything incomplete is scheduled rather than carried mentally into Monday.

Six-week provision

Food inventory and plan

The aim is to know what exists, prevent waste, control spending and make working days easier.

1. Inventory

Record freezer, fridge, cupboards, staples, expiry order and approximate portions. Photograph shelves only as an aid; the written list remains the working record.

2. Build six weekly themes

Plan breakfasts, work lunches, evening meals, Friday abstinence and simple fallback meals. Reuse ingredients across meals.

3. Cost and rotation

Use existing food first, set a weekly shopping ceiling, identify one shopping day, and review stock before buying.

Joy within means

The free-fun calendar

Financial repair must not create a fortnight made only of deprivation and chores.

  1. List free, already-paid-for and very low-cost pleasures.
  2. Choose at least three definite enjoyable events each week.
  3. Place them in the calendar before the week begins.
  4. Include different forms of restoration: outdoors, reading, languages, gardening, music, church, local exploration or time with safe people.
  5. Record whether the activity genuinely restored you or became another project, expense or late night.
Making the Experiments repeatable

The Project Test Record

Use the Latin and Spanish sessions to test the learning products while also learning.

Scope safeguard: during the fortnight, test and record. Do not rebuild every Experiment simultaneously. A reusable pattern should be adopted only after it has worked in at least two projects and more than one real or simulated commute.
