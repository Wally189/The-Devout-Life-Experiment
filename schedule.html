Suggested schedule

Twelve weeks is the minimum first cycle, not a promise of complete reform. A lesson may be extended where the reading, health needs or practical amendment require more time.

Monday — Prepare and read

Pray briefly. Read the assigned section once without marking. Note unfamiliar words or teaching requiring clarification.

Tuesday — Recall and understand

Read again with pencil. Close the book, write the teaching from memory, then reopen it and correct your account.

Wednesday — Examine

Use the lesson questions. Record concrete evidence, not a sweeping verdict on your character.

Thursday — Distinguish and amend

Identify the kind of problem and choose one proper remedy. Bring spiritual matters to prayer; seek practical or healthcare support where appropriate.

Friday and Saturday — Practise

Live the amendment. Keep brief notes on triggers, resistance, failures, help and unexpected discoveries.

Sunday — Worship and review

Attend Mass. Complete the guided journal later in the day. Decide whether to continue, adjust or move to the next lesson.

Weeks 13–16: consolidation

Repeat the lessons that exposed the most serious or persistent problems. Complete a general review, write a modest permanent rule of life, and identify matters requiring confession, spiritual direction, reconciliation, budgeting, counselling, addiction support or medical care.

Suggested sacramental rhythm

Sunday Mass is foundational. Confession should be regular and prudent, and more immediate where grave sin requires it. The experiment does not set an artificial quota: follow the Church’s teaching and sound pastoral counsel rather than anxiety or avoidance.
