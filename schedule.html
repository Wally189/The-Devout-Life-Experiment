How to use this course

Begin with the lessons. Work through one spiritual lesson each week from Monday to Sunday. Alongside it, give the Practical page about ten minutes each day to update the register, select one next action and review what remains.

The first practical setup may take thirty to sixty minutes because the journal and register need to be established. Thereafter the daily review should remain brief unless the action itself requires more time.

Monday — Prepare and read

Pray the lesson prayer. Read the assigned primary section once without marking. Note unfamiliar words or teaching requiring clarification.

Tuesday — Recall and understand

Read again with pencil. Close the book, write the teaching from memory, then reopen it and correct your account.

Wednesday — Companion reading

Read the assigned Scupoli or Quadrupani selection. Note what it adds, qualifies or emphasises.

Thursday — Church teaching and examination

Read the Catechism or Canon Law reference. Begin the lesson questions using facts rather than sweeping verdicts.

Friday and Saturday — Amendment in practice

Choose one amendment, prepare the conditions for it and practise. Keep only brief factual notes on triggers, resistance, help and failure.

Sunday — Worship and handwritten review

Attend Mass. Complete the lesson journal by hand. Decide whether to continue, adjust or move to the next week.

The daily practical accompaniment

  1. Open the paper Practical Register.
  2. Update any item whose status changed.
  3. Choose one next physical action that fits the day.
  4. Place it in time or complete it.
  5. At night, record what was completed, scheduled or redirected.

The Practical page is not a second full lesson. It is a short discipline for preventing duties and problems from remaining vague.

Optional daily Mass readings

The readings of the day may be added to morning or evening prayer when they genuinely support recollection. Keep this optional: the course already contains substantial assigned reading.

Weeks 13–16: consolidation

Repeat the lessons that exposed the most serious or persistent problems. Review the practical pattern. Only then draft or revise a provisional lay Rule of Life from practices that have proved useful and sustainable.

Suggested sacramental rhythm

Sunday Mass is foundational. Confession should be regular and prudent, and more immediate where grave sin requires it. The experiment does not set an artificial quota: follow the Church’s teaching and sound pastoral counsel rather than anxiety or avoidance.
