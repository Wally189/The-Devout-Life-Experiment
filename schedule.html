How to use the course

This is a life-change course, not a race. The first cycle is twenty-seven weeks because the books deserve time and stable amendment is normally gradual.

Choose the form that suits your present life

Spiritual track

Follow the assigned reading, week-specific questions, one amendment and Saturday review.

Practical track

Spend about ten minutes with the Practical Register or one chosen topic exercise, then carry out one suitable action.

Combined track

Use the reading to illuminate one practical matter. Do not double the workload merely because both tracks are open.

The ordinary week begins on Sunday

  1. Sunday — orient. Pray, open the lesson, mark the reading and decide which track or tracks you will use.
  2. Monday — read. Read roughly the first half slowly for the allotted time.
  3. Tuesday — continue and recall. Continue the reading; then write its central point from memory.
  4. Wednesday — companion. Read the shorter companion or Church selection.
  5. Thursday — examine. Use one or more week-specific questions, keeping facts separate from fear and assumption.
  6. Friday — amend. Prepare one small, visible action.
  7. Saturday — practise and review. Record what happened and complete the four essential journal questions.

Twenty to thirty minutes is enough

That time may be divided between reading, prayer, handwriting and practical administration. The action itself—cleaning, attending an appointment, preparing food or completing a duty—may naturally take longer.

Minimum faithful week: read the primary text, answer one question, choose one amendment and complete the short review.

The practical accompaniment

Initial notebook setup may take thirty to sixty minutes. Afterwards, ten minutes is usually enough to update the register, select one next action or work through one practical topic.

The Practical page may be used independently and ends with prayer.

Optional daily supports

Daily Mass readings, the Angelus, Rosary, language study, travel rehearsal and other supports may be added to a personal pattern where they aid prayer or duty. They are not all universal course requirements.
