How to

This is a life-change course, not a race. The first cycle is spread across many gentle weeks because the books deserve time and stable amendment is normally gradual.

Choose the form that suits your present life

Spiritual course

Follow the assigned reading, week-specific questions, one amendment and Saturday review.

Practical course

Use the notebook, daily anchors, three priorities and weekly review to regain practical control.

Combined use

Complete the spiritual work, then spend about ten minutes on the Practical course. Do not double the workload.

The ordinary week begins on Sunday

  1. Sunday — orient. Pray, open the week, mark the reading and decide whether practical work is also needed.
  2. Monday — read. Read roughly the first half slowly for the allotted time.
  3. Tuesday — continue and recall. Continue the reading; then write its central point from memory.
  4. Wednesday — companion. Read the shorter companion or Church selection.
  5. Thursday — examine. Use one or more week-specific questions, keeping facts separate from fear and assumption.
  6. Friday — amend. Prepare one small, visible action.
  7. Saturday — practise and review. Record what happened and complete the four essential journal questions.

The normal daily pattern

Allow approximately 20–30 minutes for the spiritual course: prayer, reading, recall, examination or review according to the day.

Where using the Practical course, add about ten minutes to update the notebook, choose the three priorities or review one life domain. The practical action itself may naturally take longer.

Minimum faithful week: read the primary text, answer one question, choose one amendment and complete the short review.

Optional daily supports

Optional supports may be used occasionally or tested as possible anchors. They become part of the regular pattern only when they consistently support prayer, duty, health or charity.

Possible supports

  • Daily Mass readings.
  • The Angelus at one or more traditional times.
  • The Rosary.
  • Language study or useful reading during travel.
  • Movement, preparation for tomorrow or a brief household reset.

Test them gently

Ask: does this support the principal duty, or compete with it? Is the time realistic? Does it remain possible on an ordinary day? What is the reduced version when life is difficult?

When the ordinary week cannot be followed

Repeat a week, pause one course, use the minimum version or work at your own pace. Do not rebuild the whole course during illness, disruption or discouragement.
