Pause

Illness, grief, exhaustion, relapse, work pressure, family need or simple disorder may interrupt the course. The answer is not a dramatic restart. Use a reduced pattern until stability returns.

Do not restart. Resume. The next faithful act matters more than recovering a perfect sequence.

The reduced rule

  1. Return to God briefly. Keep one short morning prayer and one short night prayer. Attend Sunday Mass where possible and appropriate.
  2. Protect the body. Eat, sleep, hydrate and take necessary medication. Suspend optional fasting or extra penance where health or prudent care requires it.
  3. Complete essentials. Identify the principal duty and one domestic necessity. Everything else may be scheduled or deferred.
  4. Ask for help. Contact the appropriate priest, doctor, counsellor, addiction service, debt adviser, employer or trusted person when the matter exceeds private effort.
  5. Keep one next action. Choose something visible and proportionate.
  6. Resume gently. Return to the current week, repeat it, pause it or use only the Practical course. Do not return automatically to Week 1.

A five-minute journal entry

  1. What has happened?
  2. What is essential today?
  3. What can safely wait?
  4. Who or what may help?
  5. What is the next faithful act?
Prompts if words are difficult

“Today is not an ordinary day because…” · “The minimum duty is…” · “I give myself permission to defer…” · “I need to contact…” · “My next act is…”

Do not make these decisions while agitated

  • Do not invent severe penances to compensate for failure.
  • Do not rewrite the whole Rule of Life late at night.
  • Do not send major relationship or workplace messages without pause and review.
  • Do not add new practices during discouragement.
  • Do not interpret a disrupted week as proof that nothing has changed.
Original prayer for this course

Lord Jesus Christ, I cannot put everything in order today. Help me to receive what is true, protect what is necessary, seek the help I need and do the next duty in peace. Preserve me from haste, despair and self-punishment. I place what remains unfinished in your hands. Amen.
